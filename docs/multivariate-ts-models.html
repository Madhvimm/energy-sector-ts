Multivariate TS Models (ARIMAX/SARIMAX/VAR)

Define the Models

In our quest to uncover the relationships within the energy sector and its economic consequences, we will use a series of statistical models that serve as the foundation for our analysis. This will help us understand both direct impacts and more complex, interdependent relationships within the datasets.

1. ARIMAX Models:

  1. (ARIMAX) Electricity Prices ~ Crude Oil Prices + Natural Gas Prices
  2. (ARIMAX) GDP ~ Crude Oil Prices + Natural Gas Prices
  3. (ARIMAX) CPI ~ Crude Oil Prices + Natural Gas Prices

2. SARIMAX Models:

  1. (SARIMAX) Electricity Prices ~ Crude Oil Prices + Natural Gas Prices + Seasonality
  2. (SARIMAX) GDP ~ Crude Oil Prices + Natural Gas Prices + Seasonality
  3. (SARIMAX) CPI ~ Crude Oil Prices + Natural Gas Prices + Seasonality

3. VAR Models:

  1. (VAR) Crude Oil Prices ~ Natural Gas Prices + GDP + CPI
  2. (VAR) Electricity Prices ~ Crude Oil Prices + Natural Gas Prices + GDP + CPI

ARIMAX / SARIMAX

We will use ARIMAX and SARIMAX models to investigate the intricate interdependencies within our economic and energy datasets. By selecting response and exogenous variables, we construct models to capture these relationships.

Manual Function 
SARIMA.c=function(p1,p2,q1,q2,P1,P2,Q1,Q2,d1,d2,data){
  
  temp=c()
  d=1
  D=1
  s=12
  
  i=1
  temp= data.frame()
  ls=matrix(rep(NA,9*95),nrow=95)
  
  
  for (p in p1:p2)
  {
    for(q in q1:q2)
    {
      for(P in P1:P2)
      {
        for(Q in Q1:Q2)
        {
          for(d in d1:d2)
       
        {
          if(p+d+q+P+D+Q<=10)
          {
            
            model<- Arima(data,order=c(p-1,d,q-1),seasonal=c(P-1,D,Q-1))
            ls[i,]= c(p-1,d,q-1,P-1,D,Q-1,model$aic,model$bic,model$aicc)
            i=i+1

          }
          
        }
      }
    }
    
  }
  
  }
  temp= as.data.frame(ls)
  names(temp)= c("p","d","q","P","D","Q","AIC","BIC","AICc")
  
  temp
  
}
